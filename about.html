FAQ

What is baby sleep coaching?
Baby sleep coaching is a gentle, structured method that teaches babies how to fall asleep independently and stay asleep through the night. As a Certified Baby Sleep Trainer and trusted Baby Sleep Expert, I create personalized, no-cry sleep plans tailored to each baby’s unique needs. My goal is to help both babies and parents sleep better—without guilt or confusion.
Why can’t a baby sleep properly during the night?
There are many reasons—overtiredness, overstimulation, poor sleep routines, or even growth-related sleep regressions. Most babies wake frequently because they haven’t yet learned how to connect sleep cycles. That’s where a Pediatric Sleep Specialist comes in—to gently guide your baby toward deeper, more consistent sleep with evidence-based strategies and emotional support.
How can you put a baby to sleep in 30 seconds?
Realistically, no healthy method can consistently put a baby to sleep in 30 seconds—but there are calming techniques that help babies settle faster over time. With proper routines, soothing cues, and infant sleep support, many babies fall asleep more easily. I help parents develop these systems based on what works for their baby’s age, temperament, and daily rhythm.
What’s the best miracle method to make a baby sleep?
There’s no one-size-fits-all “miracle,” but there is a proven path: gentle, consistent sleep habits. As a baby sleep expert, I don’t believe in quick fixes—I believe in sustainable routines that support your baby’s emotional and physical well-being. My approach combines science, empathy, and experience to create results that feel like magic.
Will my baby sleep through the night without sleep training?
Some babies eventually do, but many continue to struggle for months or even years without support. Sleep training—especially when guided by a Certified Baby Sleep Trainer—can help you and your baby get better rest much sooner, using gentle methods that feel natural and respectful. Most families I work with see progress in as little as 2–3 weeks.
Do babies really need 4 hours of nap time during the day?
Nap needs vary by age, but yes—most babies under 12 months need at least 3–4 hours of total daytime sleep. Skipping naps can lead to overtiredness and night wakings. As a Pediatric Sleep Specialist, I help parents create age-appropriate nap routines that support healthy night sleep and overall development.
